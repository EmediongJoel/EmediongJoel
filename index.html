Bike Buyers Analysis – Excel Dashboard

Project Overview

This project analyzes customer demographics and purchasing behavior to identify factors influencing bike purchases. Using Excel, I created an interactive dashboard to visualize trends and provide data-driven insights.

Dataset Description
The dataset, "Bike Buyers," includes:
1.Demographics: Age, Gender, Marital Status, Income, Region
2.Household & Lifestyle: Number of Children, Home Ownership, Number of Cars
3. Occupation & Education: Job type, Level of Education
4. Commute Distance: Distance traveled daily
5.Purchase Behavior: Whether a customer bought a bike (Yes/No)

Project Steps & Excel Implementation
1. Data Cleaning & Preparation
Checked for missing values and inconsistencies.
Transformed categorical variables for better analysis.
Created pivot tables to summarize key metrics.

2. Dashboard Creation & Visualizations
The interactive Excel dashboard includes the following visualizations:
Line Chart –Shows trends in bike purchases over time across different age range.
Line Chart with Markers–Highlights specific data points in purchasing trends.
Column Chart–Compares bike purchases across different two genders.
Slicers-Enables dynamic filtering to explore trends by region, education and marital status.

Key Insights & Findings
1. Income & Occupation Matter:
Customers with higher incomes and professional jobs are more likely to buy bikes.
Skilled manual workers also show a high likelihood of purchasing.
2. Commute Distance is Crucial:
Most bike buyers travel short distances (0-5 miles) to work.
Longer commutes reduce the likelihood of purchasing a bike.
3. Home Ownership & Car Ownership Influence Purchases:
Fewer cars = Higher bike adoption.
Homeowners are more likely to buy bikes compared to renters.
4. Regional Differences Exist:
Some regions show stronger bike adoption rates, making them ideal target markets.

Recommendations for Business Strategy
1.Target High-Income Professionals:
Focus marketing campaigns on professionals who value fitness and eco-friendly transport.
2.Leverage Commute Distance Insights:
Partner with companies to encourage bike use for short-distance employees.
3.Expand in High-Demand Regions:
4.Prioritize areas with high bike purchases while identifying growth opportunities in low-demand regions:
Introduce Financing & Discounts for Homeowners & Low-Car Owners:
Offer flexible payment plans or discounts to increase adoption rates.

Project Overview
This Power BI dashboard analyzes insights from a survey of data professionals, highlighting key trends in job roles, salaries, career entry challenges, and job satisfaction. The goal of this project is to provide a data-driven perspective on the data industry, helping aspiring professionals understand industry trends, also, businesses can use this data to identify trends and optimize hiring strategies.

What This Dashboard Shows
This dashboard breaks down survey responses into five key areas:
1.Demographics – Where respondents are from
2.Job Roles & Salaries –Average earnings across different data-related careers
2.Programming Languages–Most popular coding languages among data professionals
3.Career Entry Challenges– Difficulty level in breaking into the industry
4.Work & Salary Satisfaction–Professionals' happiness levels.

Project Steps & Implementation
1. Data Collection & Cleaning: The survey data was imported from an Excel dataset, containing multiple responses
Cleaned the data by removing duplicates, handling missing values, and standardizing formats
2. Data Modeling & Transformation (Power BI)
Used Power Query Editor to clean and transform the data.
Created calculated columns & measures to compute average salaries, job satisfaction scores, and survey distributions.
3. Visualization & Dashboard Design(Power BI):
Tree Map→Shows the distribution of survey participants by country.
Bar Chart→ Compares average salary across job
Stacked Column Chart→Displays the most popular programming languages among data professionals.
Pie Chart (Donut Chart)→ Illustrates the difficulty of entering the data industry.
KPI Cards→ Highlights total respondents (630) and average age (29.87 years).
Gauge Charts→ Measures happiness levels regarding work-life balance (5.74/10) and salary (4.27/10).
Slicers→ Enable dynamic filtering by job title and other key factors.

Key Insights & Findings
1.Salary Trends→ Data Scientists earn the highest salaries, followed by Data Engineers and Architects.
2.Programming Languages→ Python is the most widely used, reinforcing its dominance in the industry.
3.Career Challenges → 42.7% of respondents find it difficult to break into data careers.
4.Work-Life Balance vs. Salary Satisfaction→ Work-life balance satisfaction (5.74/10) is higher than salary satisfaction (4.27/10), indicating potential compensation concerns.
